ABOUT ME

GIS, remote sensing, and environmental mapping with a focus on clear communication, spatial insight, and meaningful real-world application.

I am a GIS and remote sensing specialist with a background in environmental analysis, marine mapping, and spatial communication. I am particularly interested in how geospatial methods can support stronger environmental understanding, clearer decision-making, and more meaningful connections between people and place.

My academic background combines technical geospatial training with a broader understanding of landscapes, society, and environmental change. I completed a Master’s degree in Geographic Information Systems and Remote Sensing at Ulster University, where I developed skills in GIS, spatial analysis, photogrammetry, marine remote sensing, geodesy, programming, and environmental mapping. My earlier degree in Social Geography gave me a strong foundation in spatial thinking, planning, GIS-based modelling, and data visualisation.

Professionally, I have worked with GIS, mapping, and environmental analysis through roles including Climate and Environment Advisor at SALT, a geodata placement with Sandefjord Municipality, and volunteer GIS work linked to recreational water mapping in Ireland. I have also contributed to marine and coastal projects involving SeaSketch, marine protected areas, harbour mapping, and the development of remote sensing approaches for kelp monitoring.

I enjoy combining technical analysis with visual communication, whether through map design, spatial storytelling, or project-based geospatial outputs. Alongside my current work, I am continuing to expand my competence in quantitative geospatial analysis, drone-based data collection, LiDAR, and 3D modelling. I am also interested in exploring new developments in AI and machine learning where they can strengthen geospatial workflows, interpretation, and environmental insight.
