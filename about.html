ABOUT ME

I work across GIS, remote sensing, environmental analysis, and marine mapping, with a strong interest in how geospatial methods can support better environmental understanding and decision-making. My background combines technical geospatial training with broader landscape and societal perspectives, allowing me to connect data, place, and real-world environmental challenges.

I completed a Master’s degree in Geographic Information Systems and Remote Sensing at Ulster University, with training in GIS, spatial analysis, photogrammetry, marine remote sensing, geodesy, programming, and environmental mapping. My earlier degree in Social Geography gave me a strong foundation in spatial thinking, GIS-based modelling, planning, data visualisation, and environmental interpretation.

Professionally, I have worked with GIS, mapping, and environmental analysis through roles including Climate and Environment Advisor at SALT, a geodata placement with Sandefjord Municipality, and remote volunteer GIS work linked to recreational water mapping in Ireland. I have also built project experience in marine and coastal topics including SeaSketch, marine protected areas, harbour mapping, and kelp-related remote sensing development.

I am especially interested in remote sensing, environmental monitoring, marine and coastal systems, geospatial communication, and practical applications of mapping for conservation and sustainability. I enjoy combining analysis with visual communication, and I am motivated by work that connects technical tools with meaningful environmental outcomes.
